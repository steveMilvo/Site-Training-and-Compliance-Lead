# 09 - Competency & Learning Framework
## Assessment Question
### "How would you improve competency, engagement and operational performance?"

---

# Philosophy

One of the statements in the case study immediately stood out to me.

Employees are acting in classifications without formal assessment.

For me, this represents one of the highest operational risks identified in the scenario.

Training attendance is not competency.

Years of service is not competency.

Confidence is not competency.

Competency is the demonstrated ability to consistently perform a task safely, correctly and to the required quality standard.

My objective would therefore be to move Australian Vintage from a training-based model towards a competency-based workforce.

# Adult Learning Principles

Training should always answer three questions.

Why is this important?

How do I do it?

How will I know I've done it correctly?

Adults engage more effectively when learning is immediately relevant to their work.

# Verification

Assessment should not automatically equal competency.

Verification should confirm:

Task performed correctly.

Procedure followed.

Safety maintained.

Quality achieved.

Documentation completed.

Behaviour consistent.

Supervisor satisfied.

# Long-Term Vision

My long-term objective is to create a workforce where competency is visible, measurable and continuously developing.

Employees understand their learning pathway.

Supervisors understand workforce capability.

Managers understand operational readiness.

Executives understand organisational resilience.

Training therefore becomes a strategic contributor to operational excellence rather than simply an administrative function.

Ultimately, capability becomes one of Australian Vintage's competitive advantages.
