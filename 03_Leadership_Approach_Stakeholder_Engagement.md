# 03 - Leadership Approach & Stakeholder Engagement
## Assessment Question
### "What is your leadership approach and how would you engage stakeholders?"

---

# Leadership Philosophy

Leadership is not about having the answers.

Leadership is creating an environment where the right people contribute to the right decisions.

My leadership philosophy is based on Ronald Heifetz's Adaptive Leadership model.

Effective leaders continually move between:

The Dance Floor
- Building relationships
- Observing work
- Listening
- Coaching
- Supporting people
- Understanding operational pressures

and

The Balcony
- Looking at the organisation as a system
- Identifying patterns
- Analysing data
- Understanding governance
- Prioritising strategically
- Developing long-term capability

I believe this role requires both perspectives.

---

# My Leadership Style

I describe my leadership style as:

Visible

Collaborative

Structured

Supportive

Accountable

Evidence-based

People focused

Continuous improvement focused

I believe people generally want to do good work.

If performance is inconsistent, I first ask:

Do they know what is expected?

Do they have the right skills?

Do they have the right tools?

Are procedures clear?

Is the system supporting them?

Only after understanding these questions do I consider individual performance.

# Leadership Priorities

During my first month my priorities are:

# My Leadership Commitments

I will be visible.

I will listen more than I speak.

I will seek operational evidence before making recommendations.

I will support supervisors.

I will be available.

I will communicate openly.

I will create accountability through systems rather than blame.

# Stakeholder Engagement Philosophy

Training cannot operate independently.

Learning supports operations.

Operations supports quality.

Quality supports customers.

Everyone therefore becomes a stakeholder.

My approach would be based on partnership rather than ownership.

# Managing Resistance

Resistance often indicates uncertainty rather than opposition.

My approach would be:

Listen.

Understand concerns.

Explain why.

Involve stakeholders.

Demonstrate benefits.

Implement progressively.

Review outcomes.

Adjust where appropriate.

# Leadership Outcome

My goal is not to become the owner of training.

My goal is to help Australian Vintage develop a culture where learning, competency, compliance and operational excellence become part of everyday work rather than separate activities.

When leaders, supervisors and operators all understand their role in workforce capability, sustainable improvement becomes possible.
