# 11 - Executive Dashboards, KPIs & Business Intelligence
## Assessment Question
### "How would you measure success?"

---

# Introduction

One of the biggest mistakes organisations make is measuring activity instead of performance.

Examples include:

- Number of courses delivered
- Number of training hours
- Number of attendees

These measures tell us very little about whether workforce capability has actually improved.

My philosophy is that training should ultimately improve operational performance.

Therefore the dashboard should focus on organisational capability rather than training activity.

---

# Dashboard Philosophy

Different leaders require different information.

An operator needs different information to a supervisor.

A supervisor needs different information to an Executive.

The dashboard framework should therefore provide information appropriate to each level of the organisation.

# Reporting Philosophy

Reports should answer:

Where are we today?

What changed?

What requires attention?

What decisions are needed?

Reports should drive decisions rather than simply provide information.

---

# Long-Term Vision

A mature dashboard environment allows leaders to move from reactive management to proactive leadership.

Instead of discovering capability problems after an incident, leaders can identify risks early, allocate resources effectively and make informed decisions based on reliable workforce capability data.

The ultimate goal is to create an organisation where information supports better decisions, stronger leadership and improved operational performance across every Australian Vintage business unit.
