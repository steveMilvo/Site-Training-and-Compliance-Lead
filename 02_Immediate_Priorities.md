# 02 - Immediate Priorities
## Assessment Question
### "What would your immediate priorities be?"

---

# Leadership Philosophy

In my experience, organisations under operational pressure do not need more activity.

They need clarity.

My immediate priorities would therefore focus on stabilising the business, reducing operational risk and building confidence rather than launching large improvement projects.

The objective of the first 30 days is to create stability while preparing the organisation for sustainable improvement.

---

# Priority One
# Keep People Safe

Safety will always be my first priority.

The scenario identifies:

- Near misses
- Training complacency
- Employees performing duties without formal competency
- Vintage approaching

This creates an elevated operational risk.

Immediate actions would include:

Review high-risk activities.

Identify employees currently working outside formally verified competency.

Review mandatory training.

Review permits.

Meet WHS.

Review recent incidents.

Review near misses.

Review corrective actions.

Ensure supervisors understand immediate expectations.

---

# Priority Two
# Support Vintage

Vintage is the organisation's most important operational period.

Training should never become an obstacle to production.

Instead, training should support production.

I would meet:

Operations

Cellar

Production

Packaging

Laboratory

Warehouse

to understand:

critical roles

critical competencies

critical dates

resource pressures

known capability gaps

seasonal workforce requirements

---

# Priority Three
# Build Trust

No capability framework succeeds without trust.

During the first month I would spend significant time with supervisors.

Questions would include:

What makes your job difficult?

What training actually helps?

What wastes your time?

What do your people struggle with?

How can Learning & Compliance better support production?

The objective is to become viewed as an operational partner rather than an administrator.

---

# Priority Four
# Stabilise Compliance

The scenario identifies:

Competency records inconsistent.

Skills matrices misaligned.

Employees acting in classifications without assessment.

Rather than immediately correcting records, I would first understand:

Why?

How?

Where?

Who?

Only after understanding root causes would I begin correcting records.

---

# Priority Five
# Understand Systems

I would review every system currently supporting workforce capability.

Examples include:

Learning

Competency

HR

SharePoint

Document Control

Quality

Laboratory

Maintenance

Production

Safety

The objective is understanding.

Not replacement.

---

# Priority Six
# Review Governance

Governance determines whether systems remain effective after people leave.

I would review:

Policy ownership

SOP ownership

Approval workflows

Review schedules

Version control

Competency governance

Training governance

Audit processes

Corrective action management

---

# Priority Seven
# Identify Quick Wins

I would deliberately identify improvements capable of being implemented within 30 days.

Examples may include:

Updating outdated SOPs.

Removing duplicate documents.

Correcting obvious competency gaps.

Improving communication.

Improving visibility of training.

Supervisor briefing sessions.

QR access to critical SOPs.

Improving induction material.

These improvements demonstrate progress while larger initiatives are being developed.

# Decision Making Principles

Every decision during the first month will be tested against five questions.

Does it improve safety?

Does it support production?

Does it improve quality?

Does it improve capability?

Is it sustainable?

If the answer is no to most of these questions, it is unlikely to be an immediate priority.

---

# Risks During Month One

Potential risks include:

Operational resistance.

Change fatigue.

Supervisor workload.

Vintage time pressures.

Competing priorities.

Limited resources.

Historical practices.

Lack of confidence.

These risks reinforce the importance of listening before changing.

---

# Success Measures

I would consider the first month successful if:

Operational leaders know me.

Supervisors trust me.

The Executive Team understands the current state.

High-risk competency gaps have been identified.

Immediate safety concerns have been addressed.

A practical improvement roadmap has been agreed.

The organisation has confidence that future improvements are evidence-based rather than assumptions.

---

# Transition to Medium-Term Improvement

Only after completing this discovery and stabilisation phase would I begin implementing broader improvements.

The information collected during the first month becomes the foundation for an integrated Workforce Capability Governance Framework that supports Australian Vintage's long-term operational excellence, compliance and workforce development.
