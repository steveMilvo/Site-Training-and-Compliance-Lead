# 01 - Current State Assessment
## Assessment Question
### "How would you assess the current situation?"

---

# Leadership Philosophy

My first responsibility as Site Training & Compliance Lead is not to immediately implement change.

My responsibility is to understand the business before recommending change.

The scenario presented contains numerous operational symptoms:

- Inconsistent competency records
- Skills matrices not aligned with digital systems
- Employees working in higher classifications without formal assessment
- Low to medium engagement in training
- Vintage planning underway
- Supervisors unable to release employees for training
- Near miss incidents
- Wine losses attributed to complacency
- Requirement to improve collaboration across departments
- Requirement to improve operational performance

Rather than viewing these as separate issues, I see them as indicators of a broader workforce capability governance challenge.

Before recommending solutions I would undertake a structured Workforce Capability Governance Review.

---

# Objectives

The review has five objectives.

1. Understand the business.

2. Build credibility with operational teams.

3. Understand existing systems.

4. Identify operational and compliance risk.

5. Develop evidence-based recommendations.

---

# Leadership During Week One

My first week would be spent primarily on the "Dance Floor."

I would deliberately avoid spending the week behind a computer.

Instead I would be highly visible across the site.

The purpose is to understand how work is actually performed rather than how procedures say work should be performed.

# My Initial Conclusions

At this stage I would not recommend replacing software.

I would not recommend redesigning processes.

I would not recommend changing governance.

Instead I would recommend stabilising operations while building a complete understanding of how Australian Vintage currently manages workforce capability.

Only once the evidence has been gathered would I recommend improvements.

This approach ensures recommendations are based on operational reality rather than assumptions.

---

# Success Criteria for Month One

I consider Month One successful if I have achieved:

Strong relationships with operational leaders.

Trust with supervisors.

Understanding of production.

Understanding of laboratory operations.

Understanding of quality systems.

Complete systems inventory.

Complete document governance review.

Complete competency review.

Executive Current State Report.

Evidence-based improvement roadmap.

This establishes the foundation for all future improvements and ensures subsequent decisions are aligned with Australian Vintage's operational priorities, regulatory obligations and long-term business strategy.
