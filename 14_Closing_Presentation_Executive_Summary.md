# 14 - Closing Presentation & Executive Summary
## Leadership Assessment Closing Statement

---

# Executive Summary

Over the past twenty years I have learned that organisations rarely struggle because their people lack commitment.

More often, they struggle because good people are working within systems that have gradually become fragmented.

Training becomes separated from operations.

Competency becomes separated from assessment.

Quality becomes separated from learning.

Policies become separated from practice.

Departments become separated from one another.

The scenario presented by Australian Vintage reflects many of these challenges.

Rather than seeing isolated problems, I see an opportunity to strengthen the entire Workforce Capability system.

---

# My Philosophy

People do not come to work intending to make mistakes.

When mistakes occur, leaders should first ask:

Did we provide the right information?

Did we provide the right training?

Did we assess competency?

Did we support supervisors?

Was the procedure easy to access?

Was the system simple to follow?

If the answer to any of these questions is no, then improving the system should be our first priority.

---

# What Success Looks Like

Twelve months after commencing, I would hope to see an organisation where:

Employees know exactly where to find current procedures.

Supervisors understand the capability of every member of their team.

Managers have confidence in workforce readiness.

Executives have visibility of organisational capability.

Training supports production rather than competes with production.

Quality findings continually improve learning.

Food Safety is embedded into everyday work.

Continuous Improvement becomes normal behaviour.

Learning becomes part of organisational culture.

---

# My Leadership Commitments

If appointed I commit to:

Being visible.

Being approachable.

Listening before changing.

Supporting supervisors.

Working alongside operational teams.

Making evidence-based decisions.

Communicating honestly.

Building trust.

Creating practical solutions.

Leaving the organisation stronger than I found it.

---

# Legacy

I always ask myself one question.

"If I leave this organisation in five years, will the systems continue working?"

If the answer is no, I haven't done my job properly.

My objective is to build systems that are:

Simple.

Repeatable.

Governed.

Easy to maintain.

Easy to hand over.

Capable of supporting Australian Vintage long after individual people have moved on.

---

# Why Australian Vintage?

Australian Vintage represents far more than another manufacturing organisation.

It is a complex business operating across multiple product streams with highly skilled people, significant operational capability and exciting opportunities for future growth.

The opportunity to contribute to such an organisation genuinely excites me.

I enjoy solving complex organisational problems.

I enjoy developing people.

I enjoy building systems that improve both operational performance and workplace culture.

I believe this role combines all three.

---

# Final Reflection

If successful, my first responsibility would not be to introduce change.

It would be to earn trust.

Trust with operators.

Trust with supervisors.

Trust with managers.

Trust with executives.

Only once that trust has been established can meaningful and sustainable improvement occur.

---

# My Vision

I do not see the Site Training & Compliance Lead as someone who simply coordinates courses.

I see the role as helping Australian Vintage build an organisation where:

Knowledge is captured.

Capability is visible.

Compliance is embedded.

Quality is protected.

Safety is strengthened.

People continue developing.

Leaders make better decisions.

Operational excellence becomes sustainable.

That is the organisation I would be proud to help build.

---

Thank you for the opportunity to present my thoughts.

While today's presentation responds to the assessment scenario, it also reflects the way I approach leadership.

I believe great training departments don't simply deliver learning.

They improve businesses.

They improve culture.

They improve safety.

They improve quality.
