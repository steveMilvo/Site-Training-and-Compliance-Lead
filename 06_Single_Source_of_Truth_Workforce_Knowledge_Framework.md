# 06 - Single Source of Truth & Workforce Knowledge Framework
## Assessment Question
### "How would you improve engagement, collaboration and long-term business performance?"

---

# Introduction

The scenario identifies several issues that initially appear unrelated.

- Competency records are inconsistent.
- Skills matrices are not aligned.
- Training engagement is low.
- Supervisors cannot release staff.
- Near misses have occurred.
- Wine losses have occurred.
- Departments work independently.
- Information is distributed across multiple systems.

From my perspective these are not separate problems.

They are symptoms of fragmented workforce knowledge.

The long-term opportunity is to create a Single Source of Truth for workforce capability while respecting the specialised systems already used across the business.

This is not about replacing software.

It is about improving governance.

---

# Definition

## Single Source of Truth

A Single Source of Truth (SSOT) is a governance model where employees have confidence they are always accessing the latest approved information, regardless of where that information is physically stored.

The objective is consistency.

Not centralisation for its own sake.

# Long-Term Vision

Knowledge should become an organisational asset rather than an individual asset.

No critical information should exist only in someone's memory.

When experienced employees retire or leave, their knowledge should remain available to the organisation through structured procedures, learning resources and competency frameworks.

The long-term vision is an organisation where every employee has confidence they are working from the latest approved information, every supervisor understands workforce capability, and every executive can see the organisation's operational readiness through clear, reliable governance.
